Introduction to Travel & Tourism Industry

Definition of Tourism

Tourism involves people traveling outside their usual places for business, vacation, or other purposes.

Sectors of Tourism

  • Accommodation Sector: Places where tourists can stay, ranging from basic to luxurious (e.g., hotels, resorts, guesthouses).
  • Food and Beverage Service Sector: Establishments that prepare and serve meals and drinks (e.g., restaurants, bars, cafes).
  • Attractions Sector: Sites or facilities visited for leisure or interest, including both natural and man-made attractions.
  • Transportation Sector: Services for moving people or goods (e.g., airlines, buses, ferries).
  • MICE Sector (Meetings, Incentives, Conventions, and Exhibitions): Services involved in organizing events and gatherings.

Forms of Tourism

Major Forms:

  • International Tourism: Traveling to a different country (e.g., a Filipino visiting Japan).
  • Domestic Tourism: Traveling within the same country (e.g., a Manila resident visiting Baguio).

Other Types:

  • Art Tourism: Exposure to art, such as paintings and exhibitions.
  • Business Tourism: Traveling for business purposes and using related services.
  • Culinary Tourism: Experiencing local food and dining culture.
  • Cultural/Heritage Tourism: Immersing in a place's history, religion, and traditions.
  • Dark/Grief Tourism: Visiting places associated with death or tragedy.
  • Disaster Tourism: Visiting areas affected by natural disasters.
  • Educational Tourism: Traveling to learn from places, cultures, or environments.
  • Events Tourism: Attending events, such as concerts, festivals, or sports games.
  • Extreme/Shock Tourism: Seeking thrilling or dangerous travel experiences.
  • Health Tourism: Also known as Medical Tourism; traveling to receive healthcare services.
  • Pop-Culture Tourism: Visiting locations featured in movies, books, or music.
  • Poverty Tourism (Poorism/Slum Tourism): Visiting impoverished areas to raise awareness or gain perspective.
