Concepts and Perspectives of Community

I. Theories of Social Change

Social change is the transformation of society's structure and cultural patterns. It is often seen as progress, assuming these changes benefit society.

A. Equilibrium Theory

Societies aim for internal balance (homeostasis). Changes continue until a stable state is achieved, adjusting constantly to external forces.

B. Evolutionary Theory

Societies evolve towards greater complexity to adapt to environmental and historical challenges. Progress is seen as an upward, linear movement.

II. Understanding Community Dynamics and Actions

Community dynamics involve strategic efforts that develop local capacities and resources to uplift the community and fulfill its potential.

A. Definition of Community

A community doesn't require a fixed physical location; it is a group of people connected by shared interests, identities, or goals.

1. Social Science Perspective

Communities vary in shape, size, and nature. Each is unique. Communities are more than just their members—they include relationships and structures.

2. Institutional Perspective

Communities can emerge through professional collaboration across institutions, adapting to modern societal changes.

3. Civil Society Perspective

Communities consist of interconnected social groups that collaborate to share resources, culture, and goals in an organized way.

4. Local Community

These communities share physical environments and social traits such as beliefs, needs, or risks that influence group identity and cohesion.

5. Grassroots Community

A movement driven by the people of a community, often politically or socially motivated. It begins locally, sometimes influencing broader societal change.

MODULE 2

Introduction to Literary Genres

I. Elements of Poetry

  • Verse: A line in a poem. Groups of lines form stanzas, much like paragraphs in prose.
  • Rhyme: The repetition of ending sounds in words, creating a rhyme scheme.
  • Meter: The pattern of stressed and unstressed syllables, contributing to the rhythm.
  • Theme: The central message or idea of the poem.
  • Imagery: Descriptive language that creates sensory experiences.

II. Elements of Fiction/Nonfiction

  • Plot: The sequence of events, including:
    • Introduction: Introduces characters, setting, and conflict.
    • Middle: Rising action and complications.
    • Climax: The turning point and most intense moment.
    • Resolution: The conclusion where conflicts are resolved.
  • Point of View (POV):
    • First Person: Narrator uses "I" or "we".
    • Second Person: Uses "you". Rarely used.
    • Third Person: Uses "he", "she", or "they".
      • Limited: Restricted to one character's perspective.
      • Omniscient: All-knowing narrator.
  • Character:
    • Major (Protagonist) and Minor (Observer) roles.
    • Complex: Multi-dimensional and evolving.
    • Simple: Static and less impactful.
  • Setting: Time and place of the story.
  • Tone and Atmosphere: Tone reflects the author's attitude; atmosphere is the mood.
  • Symbol: Represents deeper meaning beyond the literal.
  • Irony:
    • Verbal: Saying the opposite of what is meant.
    • Situational: Unexpected outcomes.
    • Dramatic: Reader knows more than the character.
  • Theme and Meaning: The central idea and its impact on the reader.

III. Elements of Drama

  • Characters: List of roles and a narrator, if present.
  • Setting: Locations and times where events take place.
  • Descriptions: Background and context information.
  • Dialogue: Characters’ spoken lines revealing thoughts and actions.
  • Stage Direction: Instructions for movement and delivery.
  • Theme: Central message shown through characters and plot.

IV. Conventions of Traditional Genre

  • Literary Conventions: Recognizable features of genres:
    • Greek Tragedy: Includes a chorus.
    • Fable: Ends with a moral lesson.
    • Villanelle: Poem with repeating lines and strict structure.
    • Play: Relies on dialogue and directions, unlike prose forms.
  • Archetypes (Universal Symbols): Appear in characters, settings, and themes:
    • Characters: Hero, Mother, Monster.
    • Settings: Castle, Forest, Sea.
    • Themes: Good vs. Evil, Love conquers all, Overcoming hardships.
