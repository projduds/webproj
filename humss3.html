Introduction to Creative Writing

Creative writing is writing that expresses the writer’s thoughts and feelings in an imaginative, often unique, poetic way.

It is a form of artistic expression, drawing on the imagination to convey meaning through imagery, narrative, and drama. This contrasts with analytic or pragmatic forms of writing.

This genre includes poetry, fiction (novels, short stories), scripts, screenplays, and creative non-fiction.

Creative writing goes beyond normal professional, academic, or technical literature, characterized by an emphasis on narrative craft, character development, and literary tropes, or traditions of poetry and poetics.

Technical writing, on the other hand, is a form of writing about a particular subject that requires instruction, direction, or explanation. It serves different purposes and has different characteristics than creative, academic, or professional writing.

Trends, Networks, and Critical Thinking

Trend

A trend is a general development or change in behavior, opinion, or culture over time. It may also refer to popular topics on social media. Trends reflect long-term shifts in society and are more than temporary interests.

I. Elements and Characteristics of a Trend

A. Elements of a Trend

  • Unique Content: Originality attracts attention and increases the chances of something becoming a trend.
  • Timing: Launching ideas at the right moment is key to maximizing impact.
  • Virality: A trend spreads quickly and becomes widely known.
  • Compelling Influencers: Trusted individuals or groups (e.g., celebrities) help spread trends effectively.
  • Correct Distribution Network: Using the right platform (e.g., TikTok, Instagram) to spread content rapidly.
  • Longevity: Trends that last a long time can lead to popularity and financial success.

B. Characteristics of a Trend

  • Remains popular for a long duration.
  • Accepted across various industries and cultures.
  • Often rooted in shared traditions, beliefs, and values.
  • Shows a noticeable rise or fall in public interest.

II. The Process of Identifying a Trend

A. How to Spot Trends

  • Deep Research: Go beyond surface-level searches; analyze different societal perspectives.
  • Real Proof: Observe and engage with diverse groups to understand emerging behaviors.
  • Avoid Bias: Do not let personal opinions influence your judgment when identifying trends.

III. Differentiating a Trend from a Fad

A. Definition of Fad

  • A style or practice that gains brief popularity and fades quickly.

B. Trend vs. Fad (Illustrated)

  • Trends: Long-term popularity, reflect societal value shifts.
    Example: Denim/blue jeans — popular for over 150 years, evolving from workwear to high fashion.
  • Fads: Temporary and style-based popularity.
    Examples: Skinny jeans, acid-wash jeans, high-rise styles.
